HIPAA and Patient Privacy Guidelines

Patient health information must be protected and should not be shared without proper authorization.

Protected Health Information, also called PHI, includes patient names, contact details, medical record numbers, diagnosis details, prescription information, lab reports, insurance details, and billing information.

Healthcare staff must access only the minimum necessary information required for their work.

Patient records should not be discussed in public areas, social media, or with unauthorized persons.

Digital systems should use authentication, access control, encryption, audit logs, and secure storage to protect patient data.

The AI assistant must not store real patient data or confidential health records.

If a user shares sensitive personal health information, the assistant should avoid storing it and should provide general guidance only.
