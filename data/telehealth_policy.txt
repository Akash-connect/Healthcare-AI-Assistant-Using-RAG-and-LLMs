Telehealth Consultation Guidelines

Patients may use telehealth services for follow-up consultations, minor symptoms, chronic disease management, prescription review, and general medical guidance.

Telehealth consultations are not suitable for medical emergencies, severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, stroke symptoms, major injuries, or conditions requiring physical examination.

Patients must provide accurate symptoms, current medications, allergies, and medical history during the consultation.

Medication refill requests may be reviewed during telehealth visits if the medication was previously prescribed and does not require an in-person evaluation.

Controlled substances, new high-risk medications, and medications requiring physical tests may require an in-person visit.

Patients should contact emergency services immediately in case of urgent or life-threatening symptoms.
